RegSeqDB will serve as the primary database for all data related to a preprint by Röschinger, et al. and the subsequent analysis performed by the Galagan lab. Researchers in the Phillips Lab sought to identify condition dependent regulatory elements within E. coli by performing Reg-Seq, which consisted of introducing random mutations into promoter sequences and measuring the effects on downstream gene expression under various experimental conditions [1]. The Galagan lab then utilized biophysical models of transcription factor (TF) and RNA polymerase (RNAP) binding (BoltzNet and Promoter Calculator, respectively) to predict TF/RNAP binding sites and their corresponding binding energies [2,3]. Current analyses are exploring the use of these biophysical quantities to predict gene expression. The data contained in this database will provide easy access to the promoter sequences, their corresponding RNA and DNA barcode counts under different experimental conditions, and all TF and RNAP binding sites/energies. The ultimate goal of this database is to facilitate easy and reliable access to the data so that downstream analyses are repeatable. Future analyses are also likely to grow in complexity and scale as additional models for transcription factor binding are integrated into BoltzNet, highlighting the project’s need for structured and easily expandable databases.
